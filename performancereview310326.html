What Delivered Results

  • Lead volume up 18.8% month on month with 19 Meta-attributed leads in March vs 16 in February
  • CPL improved 3.6% from $189.47 to $182.72 despite rising auction costs
  • Click volume surged 35.4% (2,855 vs 2,108) driven by Phase 2 creative efficiency
  • Landing page views up 22.4% (1,365 vs 1,115) showing stronger funnel entry
  • HubSpot pipeline showing 7 Discovery Calls, 1 Actively Working, 1 at 3rd Pricing Call, and 1 new Customer signed

What Needs Attention

  • CPM escalated 41.7% month on month and 110% from Feb to latest week, indicating severe auction pressure and creative fatigue
  • Impressions dropped 19.2% despite higher spend, meaning we are paying significantly more for less reach
  • CBO continues to misallocate budget. Ad 8 absorbed 61.5% of March spend, then Ad 3 P2 took 55.9% in the latest week
  • Frequency rising from 1.32 in Feb to 1.59 in latest week, audience saturation building
  • Only 5 ads active in the latest week down from the full creative set earlier in the month

Key Finding

March was a structurally sound month on the surface: more leads, lower CPL, stronger click efficiency. But the underlying signals tell a different story. CPM has risen 110% since February, impressions are declining despite higher spend, and CBO is cycling through ads at an unsustainable rate. Ad 8 dominated early March then fatigued. Ad 3 P2 absorbed budget in the latest week at $97.70 CPM. The account needs structural intervention this week to avoid a CPL blowout in April.

The Divergence Problem

Clicks and CTR improved significantly while impressions and reach declined. This means the creative is working harder per impression, but we are buying fewer impressions at a much higher price. The algorithm is finding engaged users but the auction cost to reach them has nearly doubled. This is the classic fatigue compression pattern: fewer impressions, higher CPM, stronger click rates from a narrowing responsive segment.

Why CPL Still Improved

CPL dropped from $189.47 to $182.72 because Phase 2 creative brought fresh engagement that more than offset the rising auction costs. The 67.6% CTR improvement drove more landing page views per dollar despite the CPM increase. This efficiency gain is temporary. As Phase 2 creative matures and CPM continues climbing, CPL will start rising unless we address the structural issues.

Budget Structure Recommendation

The single most impactful change we can make right now is moving from CBO to ad-level budget control. CBO has proven repeatedly that it cannot self-optimise at 4 leads per week. It finds one winner, pours all budget into it until CPM spikes, then the ad fatigues and we start over with no backup creative warmed up. Manual budget distribution across 4 to 5 ads at $20 to $25 each gives every creative a fair shot while preventing the concentration death spiral.

Creative Pipeline Priority

At current fatigue rates, we need fresh creative every 3 to 4 weeks. We are currently running on Phase 2 assets that launched mid-March. If we do not have Phase 3 ready by mid-April, we risk a repeat of the Phase 1 fatigue pattern where CPM doubled within 4 weeks. Filming session scheduling this week is critical.

  • 19 leads at $182.72 CPL, 54% below $400 target

  • CTR surged 67.6% month on month showing strong message market fit
  • HubSpot pipeline strengthened with 7 Discovery Calls and 1 at 3rd Pricing Call

What Needs Fixing

  • CPM escalation from $31.50 to $66.27 is unsustainable and will erode CPL gains if unchecked
  • CBO budget hoarding continues to be the structural problem. It burned Ad 8, now burning Ad 3 P2
  • Impression decline of 19.2% despite higher spend means we are paying more for less audience
  • Phase 3 creative pipeline needs to be initiated now to prevent another fatigue cycle
  • Client close rate of ~4.5% is below the 10% benchmark. Pipeline is strong but conversion to customer needs attention on the sales side

Priority Actions

  • Restructure from CBO to manual budget allocation this week
  • Force spend to Ad 1 P2, Ad 2 P2, and Ad 5 P2 which have the best untapped potential
  • Cap Ad 3 P2 spend to halt CPM inflation
  • Schedule Phase 3 filming with Eddy for mid-April
  • Book client review call to align on pipeline close rate strategy and creative direction for Phase 3

Strategic Position

The campaign is performing well within target economics. At $182.72 CPL against a $400 target, we have significant headroom even as CPM rises. The critical intervention needed is structural, not strategic: the ad account architecture needs to change from CBO to manual budget control, and the creative production pipeline needs to run ahead of the fatigue curve rather than reacting to it. If we make these two changes this week, the path to 20+ leads per month at sub-$200 CPL through Q2 is achievable. The HubSpot pipeline is the strongest it has been since launch with 10 active contacts and a 3rd Pricing Call opportunity that could close any day.
