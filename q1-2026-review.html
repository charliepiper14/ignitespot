Q1 2026 was the first full quarter of Meta Ads activity for Ignite Spot. The campaign scaled from a late-December 2025 launch to consistent daily delivery at $103/day across 90 days. The account generated 57 leads according to Meta's attribution, while HubSpot recorded 158 new contacts during the same period. Of those, 11 contacts converted to Customer status across 7 unique companies, with additional prospects actively progressing through the sales pipeline.
Ad 8 remained the dominant volume driver throughout Q1, capturing 61.7% of total budget and generating 68.4% of all Meta-attributed leads at a CPL of $146.21. CPL vs Target

The breakeven CPL target is $400, based on a 12-month LTV payback model ($1,199 first-year average retainer value with a 30% MQL-to-customer conversion rate). Every attribution method shows Q1 CPL significantly below this threshold.
Meta's CPL of $163.91 is the most conservative measure (smallest lead count, highest per-lead cost). Even at this level, the campaign is generating leads at 59% below breakeven. The HubSpot-blended CPL of $59.13 (all 158 contacts) is the most generous interpretation. Reality sits somewhere between these two figures.
The customer acquisition cost (CAC) of $1,334.71 per unique company ($9,343 / 7 companies) is more meaningful for evaluating true ROI. Against an average LTV of $70,300 per customer and a first-year retainer value of $22,200 (12 x $1,850), this represents a 16.6x first-year ROAS on customer acquisition. This is exceptionally strong for B2B accounting services.
Critically, several Q1 leads are still progressing through the pipeline. The 18 active prospects in Discovery, Actively Working, and Pricing stages represent additional potential customer conversions that would further improve the Q1 CAC and ROAS calculations.

CBO BUDGET CONCENTRATION
The Recurring Structural Challenge
Campaign Budget Optimisation (CBO) is Meta's default budget distribution method. The algorithm decides which ads receive budget based on predicted conversion likelihood. In theory, this maximises results. In practice, with small conversion volumes (57 leads across 90 days = 0.63 per day), the algorithm lacks sufficient signal and defaults to concentrating budget in whichever ad has the earliest positive data.
This is not a bug. It is how CBO behaves with limited conversion data. The pattern has repeated three times since launch:
Dec 2025 to Feb 2026
Ad 8 absorbed the majority of budget during the initial learning phase, establishing itself as the default delivery vehicle.
Feb to Mar 2026
Ad 8 continued at 61.7% of spend. Phase 1 supporting creatives (Ads 4, 5, 6, 7) received enough to show CTR data but not enough to build conversion signal.
Late Mar to Apr 2026
Ad 3 - P2 launched and quickly captured 91.6% of budget, repeating the same concentration pattern with a new creative.

What We Are Doing About It

The solution is not to fight CBO entirely but to manage it actively. Our approach for Q2 includes:
1. Cost cap implementation on Phase 2 ads to force more even budget distribution. By capping the cost-per-result target, we prevent any single ad from running away with budget unchecked.
2. Monitoring impression distribution daily during the first 2 weeks of new creative launches. If any ad exceeds 70% of total spend, we intervene by adjusting cost caps or pausing/restarting to reset the algorithm's allocation.
3. Building a creative pipeline so we always have fresh ads entering the system. The goal is to have 3 to 5 active creatives receiving meaningful budget at any given time.
4. Considering ABO (Ad Set Budget Optimisation) for Phase 2 testing. By giving Phase 2 its own dedicated budget, we remove the competition with Ad 8 for the same CBO pool.

Understanding the April CPM Spike

The $136.10 CPM in the first week of April looks alarming in isolation. Context matters here.
The primary driver is CBO budget concentration. Ad 3 - P2 consumed 91.6% of all spend, meaning Meta was bidding aggressively for a narrower pool of impressions through a single ad. When one ad receives almost all budget, the auction dynamics shift: Meta bids higher per impression to maintain delivery velocity, inflating CPM.
The counterpoint is that CPL dropped from $207.85 to $60.68 in the same period. The algorithm is paying more per impression but converting at a significantly higher rate, which means the net cost per lead actually improved despite the CPM spike.
The risk is sustainability. If this CPM level persists as the ad's conversion rate naturally declines over time (creative fatigue), CPL will increase. The window to diversify budget across Phase 2 creatives is now, while Ad 3 - P2 is hot and generating strong conversion data.
CPM escalation is the leading indicator of creative fatigue. It appears before conversion volume drops. We saw this with Ad 8 in late Q1 and we will see it with Ad 3 - P2 eventually. The strategic response is to always have the next wave of creative ready before the current one peaks.

Monitor CPM weekly. If the account average exceeds $80 sustained over 14 days, trigger creative refresh or restructuring. The Q1 average of $44.81 is the baseline. The April spike to $136.10 is acceptable short-term due to strong CPL but not sustainable long-term.

18 active prospects in the pipeline represent significant near-term revenue. Coordination with the sales team on follow-up timing and messaging can accelerate conversions from this existing pipeline without additional ad spend.

SCALING OPPORTUNITIES
Where the Growth Is
Budget Increase Potential
At $120/day, the current budget is conservative for B2B Meta Ads in the USA. With a proven CPL of $60.68 (most recent 7 days) and a $400 breakeven target, there is significant headroom to increase spend. A staged increase to $150/day in April, $180/day in May, and $200/day in June would scale lead volume while maintaining CPL discipline. Each $30/day increase adds approximately $900/month in spend and, at current conversion rates, 10 to 15 additional leads per month.
Phase 2 Creative Expansion
Ad 3 - P2 (It's Not Your Fault) has demonstrated breakout potential with 14 leads in 7 days. The other Phase 2 scripts (The Real Time Zone, The Sunday Scaries, The Ghost Accountant, The Midnight Calculator) need budget allocation to determine if they can replicate this performance. If even one additional Phase 2 ad performs at a similar level, total lead volume could double without proportional spend increases.
Landing Page Optimisation
The Unbounce landing page at get.ignitespot.com/quote has a reported conversion rate of 3.98%. Meta data shows 2,920 landing page views generating 57 leads, implying a 1.95% conversion rate from ad traffic specifically. Closing the gap between the overall 3.98% and the ad-specific 1.95% through A/B testing of the ad-to-page message match could significantly improve CPL without additional spend.
Retargeting Layer
With 143,707 unique users reached in Q1 and 2,920 landing page visitors, there is now a meaningful retargeting audience available. A dedicated retargeting campaign targeting landing page visitors who did not convert could capture additional leads at a lower CPL than cold prospecting.

Funnel Drop-Off Analysis

The biggest drop-off in the funnel is between Link Clicks (5,696) and Landing Page Views (2,920). Only 51.3% of people who click the ad actually load the landing page. This is typical for Meta Ads (50 to 60% click-to-LPV rate is standard) but represents an opportunity. Page load speed, mobile optimisation, and the initial above-the-fold experience all affect this number.
The second major drop-off is Landing Page Views (2,920) to Meta Leads (57), a 1.95% conversion rate. The reported Unbounce conversion rate of 3.98% includes all traffic sources. Ad traffic typically converts at a lower rate than organic or direct traffic because the intent level is different. Closing this gap through better ad-to-page message match is a high-leverage Q2 initiative.
The lead-to-customer conversion rate is strong. Of 158 Q1 contacts, 11 became customers (7.0%) and an additional 18 are actively in the pipeline (11.4%). Combined, 18.4% of Q1 contacts are either customers or active prospects, which is well above typical B2B benchmarks of 10 to 15%.
